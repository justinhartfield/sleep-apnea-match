Sleep Apnea Surgery Costs Without Insurance: 2026 Price Guide

Complete breakdown of sleep apnea surgery costs without insurance, from $3,000 nasal procedures to $100,000 jaw surgery.

Medically Reviewed by Dr. Igor I. Bussel, MD

Board-Certified Physician | UCI Gavin Herbert Eye Institute

Last reviewed and updated: January 2026

The cost of sleep apnea surgery without insurance can vary widely, with prices ranging from under $10,000 to over $100,000. The specific cost depends on the type of procedure performed, the surgeon's fees, and the location of the surgery. For example, less complex procedures like a Uvulopalatopharyngoplasty (UPPP) or nasal surgery can cost up to $10,000. These surgeries aim to remove physical obstructions in the airway.

More involved surgeries carry a higher price tag. For instance, Hypoglossal nerve stimulation therapy, such as the Inspire device, can cost between $30,000 and $40,000. This includes the cost of the device itself, which is approximately $20,000. A more extensive surgery like maxillomandibular advancement (MMA), which involves repositioning the jaw, can cost anywhere from $20,000 to $100,000. It's important for patients to consult with their healthcare providers to get a precise cost estimate based on their individual needs and the recommended surgical approach.
